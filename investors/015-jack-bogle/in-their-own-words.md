# John C. "Jack" Bogle - In Their Own Words

As of 2026-06-24, John C. Bogle is deceased. This file uses Bogle's own speeches, congressional testimony, SEC comments, book excerpts, interviews, and Q&A transcripts; it avoids unsourced quote aggregators and treats book excerpts as limited primary windows rather than a substitute for page-level book verification.

## Source note

Bogle was not a partner-letter investor in the Buffett or Marks sense. His primary corpus is public: speeches, regulatory comments, testimony, books, interviews, and Vanguard-founder retrospectives. For quote provenance, I prioritized source-visible Bogle text and kept each quotation to 25 words or fewer. Where Bogle himself was quoting another author, judge, investor, or interviewer, I excluded it from the quote index unless the line was clearly Bogle's own formulation.

## Annotated Primary-Materials Index

### Speeches And Essays

- ["The First Index Mutual Fund" (1997)](https://johncbogle.com/speeches/JCB_first_index_mf.pdf) - The compact origin story for the first retail index fund, the cost argument, the "Bogle's folly" reception, and the warning that index funds could become hot products.
- ["Investing with Simplicity" (1999)](https://johncbogle.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2019/08/Investing-with-Simplicity-1-30-99.pdf) - Core public statement of Bogle's simple-investing temperament: balance, market fluctuation, long-term thinking, and cost control.
- ["The Vanguard Story: Luck, Leadership, and Strategy" (2000)](https://johncbogle.com/speeches/JCB_NYU_10-19-00.pdf) - Best first-person narrative of Bogle's Princeton thesis, Wellington mistake, firing, Vanguard's birth, and structure-following-strategy lesson.
- ["In Investing, You Get What You Don't Pay For" (2005)](https://johncbogle.com/speeches/JCB_MS0205.pdf) - Useful source for expected-return arithmetic, investment versus speculative return, and Bogle's mid-2000s caution after the dot-com bust.
- ["The Fiduciary Principle: No Man Can Serve Two Masters" (2009)](https://johncbogle.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2010/04/JPM-Fid-Principle-Fall-2009.pdf) - Strongest single essay for his moral framing of investment management as trusteeship rather than product sales.
- [American Philosophical Society remarks (2012)](https://johncbogle.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2012/11/APS_11-10-12.pdf) - Bogle's mature synthesis of Keynes, Samuelson, enterprise, speculation, return sources, and costs.

### Books And Book Excerpts

- [The Little Book of Common Sense Investing, chapter 1 excerpt (2007)](https://johncbogle.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2007/03/chapter%20one.pdf) - Primary book excerpt for the Gotrocks parable, the Helpers metaphor, and the investor-versus-intermediary conflict.
- [The Clash of the Cultures, chapter 9 excerpt (2012)](https://johncbogle.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2013/05/c09.pdf) - Primary excerpt for Bogle's ten rules, broad-market index-fund preference, ETF warning, and fiduciary-duty policy prescription.
- [The Bogle eBlog book page for The Little Book (2007)](https://johncbogle.com/wordpress/2007/03/02/john-bogles-newest-book/) - Source trail for the posted chapter and errata context; useful as a provenance page rather than a quote source.

### Regulatory Letters, Comments, And Testimony

- [SEC proxy-vote disclosure comment (2002)](https://www.sec.gov/rules/proposed/s73602/jcbogle1.htm) - Primary comment letter for shareholder-owner logic, agent-principal language, and Bogle's insistence on mutual-fund voting transparency.
- [House Financial Services testimony (2003)](https://financialservices.house.gov/media/pdf/031203jb.pdf) - Long primary testimony on cost disclosure, Vanguard's at-cost structure, mutual-fund economics, and the cost gap between investors and intermediaries.
- [SEC fiduciary-duty submission (2013)](https://www.sec.gov/comments/4-606/4606-3097.pdf) - Primary late-career regulatory letter for the broker/adviser/fund fiduciary standard and the "two masters" conflict.

- [Ask Jack eBlog Q&A (2007)](https://johncbogle.com/wordpress/2007/03/29/john-bogle-responds-to-ask-jack-questions/) - Best concise source for Bogle's nuanced ETF position: broad, low-cost, buy-and-hold ETFs can be fine; narrow trading products are dangerous.
- [ABC News / USA Today interview (2012)](https://abcnews.com/Business/vanguards-jack-bogle-financial-train-wreck-looms/story?id=17137130) - Interview source for ETF speculation, adviser inactivity, and fiduciary-standard advocacy.

## Attribution Watchlist

- "Don't look for the needle in the haystack. Just buy the haystack." This is widely associated with Bogle and likely traces to his books, but I did not use it here because the opened primary excerpt did not contain the exact line.
- "The miracle of compounding returns is overwhelmed by the tyranny of compounding costs." AQR carries a close first-person version, but the exact famous wording needs page-level tracing before reuse.
- "Enough." Bogle popularized the Joseph Heller/Kurt Vonnegut anecdote in his book Enough, but the one-word line is not originally Bogle's; use only with that provenance.
- "Time is your friend; impulse is your enemy." Commonly attributed to Bogle online. Not used here because I did not locate a source-visible primary occurrence during this run.
- Quote aggregators were not used. Their Bogle pages often mix Bogle's own words, book subtitles, paraphrases, Buffett/Bogle cross-quotes, and Bogle quoting others.

## Reading Notes For Later Tasks

Bogle's own words show that his edge was not hidden security insight. It was repeatable arithmetic joined to institutional design. The recurring verbs are reduce, hold, serve, disclose, and simplify. The recurring enemy is not volatility but intermediation without fiduciary alignment. His most useful transfer for individual investors is behavioral: own broad markets at low cost, hold a risk level one can endure, and resist the invitation to turn a long-term plan into activity. His most difficult transfer is institutional: Bogle's shareholder-owned Vanguard structure, regulatory approvals, and scale economies cannot be copied by a household investor.

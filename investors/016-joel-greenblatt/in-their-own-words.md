## Valuation and Business Ownership

- "basic value investing" - explaining the Magic Formula as grounded in Graham and Buffett ([Forbes briefing book, 2010](https://images.forbes.com/media/pdfs/2010/04/Joel_Greenblatt_Briefing_Book.pdf)).
- "get a lot of earnings" - his plain-English description of measuring cheapness ([Forbes briefing book, 2010](https://images.forbes.com/media/pdfs/2010/04/Joel_Greenblatt_Briefing_Book.pdf)).
- "earnings to price" - the simplified cheapness measure before Gotham's adjustments ([Forbes briefing book, 2010](https://images.forbes.com/media/pdfs/2010/04/Joel_Greenblatt_Briefing_Book.pdf)).
- "return on tangible capital" - the quality side of the formula ([Forbes briefing book, 2010](https://images.forbes.com/media/pdfs/2010/04/Joel_Greenblatt_Briefing_Book.pdf)).
- "pay a lot less" - Greenblatt's compressed definition of value investing ([Meb Faber transcript, 2020](https://mebfaber.com/2020/10/28/episode-260-joel-greenblatt-gotham-asset-management-nine-out-of-ten-families-in-the-top-fifth-of-the-income-chain-have-retirement-savings-nine-in-ten-in-the-bottom-fifth-have-none/)).
- "ownership shares of businesses" - his reminder that stocks are claims on businesses, not factor labels ([Meb Faber transcript, 2020](https://mebfaber.com/2020/10/28/episode-260-joel-greenblatt-gotham-asset-management-nine-out-of-ten-families-in-the-top-fifth-of-the-income-chain-have-retirement-savings-nine-in-ten-in-the-bottom-fifth-have-none/)).
- "normalized cash flows" - what he says he uses to value businesses ([Meb Faber transcript, 2020](https://mebfaber.com/2020/10/28/episode-260-joel-greenblatt-gotham-asset-management-nine-out-of-ten-families-in-the-top-fifth-of-the-income-chain-have-retirement-savings-nine-in-ten-in-the-bottom-fifth-have-none/)).
- "causation" - his distinction between valuation logic and historical factor correlation ([Meb Faber transcript, 2020](https://mebfaber.com/2020/10/28/episode-260-joel-greenblatt-gotham-asset-management-nine-out-of-ten-families-in-the-top-fifth-of-the-income-chain-have-retirement-savings-nine-in-ten-in-the-bottom-fifth-have-none/)).

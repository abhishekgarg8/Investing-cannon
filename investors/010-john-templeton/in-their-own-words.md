## Evidence Boundaries and Guiding Questions

This file is a quote-provenance map, not a decorative quotation page. Templeton is heavily quoted on investing sites, but many famous lines are recycled without original venue, date, or context. The quote index below therefore favors short source-visible snippets from Templeton's 1993 "16 Rules for Investment Success," the John Templeton Foundation's official biography, William Green's 1999 Money profile with direct Templeton comments, and transcript mirrors that clearly identify the underlying interview but still need original-archive verification. Each quote is kept to 25 words or fewer, with source and year attached.

Guiding questions for this task were: Which Templeton phrases are visible in primary or near-primary sources? Which famous lines are actually borrowed from Graham, Baruch, Will Rogers, or later interpreters? How should the Canon label transcript mirrors and publisher pages when original books or videos are not fully text-accessible? Which materials should later F-key-writings work read in full? Did any current legal or reputational development change the framing of Templeton's own words as of this run?

Current-status check: John Templeton died in 2008, and this run found no current personal legal or regulatory proceeding involving him. A current 2026 SEC order involving Franklin's Western Asset Management and former CIO Stephen Kenneth Leech is a posthumous Franklin/Western Asset matter, not Templeton personal conduct; it belongs only in source-quality caveats when discussing the modern Franklin Templeton franchise ([John Templeton Foundation, 2026](https://www.templeton.org/about/sir-john-templeton); [SEC Western Asset order, 2026](https://www.sec.gov/files/litigation/opinions/2026/ia-6969.pdf)).

## Quote Index by Theme

### Contrarian Entry and Exit

1. "Buy low" - Templeton, 1993 ([Franklin Templeton reprint](https://www.franklintempleton.com/forms-literature/download/TL-R16)).  
   The compact rule is easy to quote and hard to execute; Templeton immediately tied it to buying when sellers are exhausted.

2. "buy when everyone else is selling" - Templeton, 1993 ([Franklin Templeton reprint](https://www.franklintempleton.com/forms-literature/download/TL-R16)).  
   This is the psychological core of the method: price opportunity appears before emotional comfort.

3. "things look darkest" - Templeton, 1993 ([Franklin Templeton reprint](https://www.franklintempleton.com/forms-literature/download/TL-R16)).  
   The line belongs with his buying-low discussion, not as a free-standing slogan detached from research.

4. "same results as everyone else" - Templeton, 1993 ([Franklin Templeton reprint](https://www.franklintempleton.com/forms-literature/download/TL-R16)).  
   He linked crowding directly to mediocre outcomes: owning consensus securities after they are popular is not an edge.

5. "Buy value" - Templeton, 1993 ([Franklin Templeton reprint](https://www.franklintempleton.com/forms-literature/download/TL-R16)).  
   Templeton meant individual security value, not market forecasts or economic headlines.

6. "market of stocks" - Templeton, 1993 ([Franklin Templeton reprint](https://www.franklintempleton.com/forms-literature/download/TL-R16)).  
   This phrase anchors his bottom-up method: macro cycles matter, but security-level value decides.

7. "maximum pessimism" - Templeton, official biography, current as of 2026 ([John Templeton Foundation](https://www.templeton.org/about/sir-john-templeton)).  
   The Foundation uses this phrase to describe his search for nations, industries, and companies at rock-bottom prices.

8. "maximum optimism" - Templeton maxims, source mirror, 2001/Templeton Touch provenance ([Novel Investor](https://novelinvestor.com/maxims-john-templeton/)).  
   The mirror pairs maximum pessimism as an entry point with maximum optimism as a sell signal.

9. "buy what most investors are selling" - Templeton maxims, source mirror, 2001/Templeton Touch provenance ([Novel Investor](https://novelinvestor.com/maxims-john-templeton/)).  
   This is one of the better operational versions of the contrarian rule because it points to supply pressure.

10. "despondently selling" - Templeton maxims, source mirror, 2001/Templeton Touch provenance ([Novel Investor](https://novelinvestor.com/maxims-john-templeton/)).  
    The emotional adjective matters: Templeton's best opportunities came when sellers were not calmly reallocating but capitulating.

11. "greedily buying" - Templeton maxims, source mirror, 2001/Templeton Touch provenance ([Novel Investor](https://novelinvestor.com/maxims-john-templeton/)).  
    His sell discipline is the mirror image of his entry rule: popularity changes the opportunity-cost math.

12. "short-term owners" - Templeton maxims, source mirror, 2001/Templeton Touch provenance ([Novel Investor](https://novelinvestor.com/maxims-john-templeton/)).  
    This phrase is useful because it identifies a specific forced-flow mechanism rather than generic pessimism.

### Global Search, Flexibility, and Diversification

13. "Remain flexible and open-minded" - Templeton, 1993 ([Franklin Templeton reprint](https://www.franklintempleton.com/forms-literature/download/TL-R16)).  
    Flexibility was not style drift for Templeton; it was the discipline of letting value determine asset class, country, and timing.

14. "no one kind of investment" - Templeton, 1993 ([Franklin Templeton reprint](https://www.franklintempleton.com/forms-literature/download/TL-R16)).  
    He rejected permanent attachment to a favored security type, industry, country, or asset class.

15. "search worldwide" - Templeton, 1993 ([Franklin Templeton reprint](https://www.franklintempleton.com/forms-literature/download/TL-R16)).  
    The global hunt was his structural edge: a wider opportunity set increases the chance of finding neglected bargains.

16. "more bargains" - Templeton, 1993 ([Franklin Templeton reprint](https://www.franklintempleton.com/forms-literature/download/TL-R16)).  
    The same rule says global search can reveal both more bargains and better bargains than a single-country search.

17. "safety of diversification" - Templeton maxims, source mirror, 2001/Templeton Touch provenance ([Novel Investor](https://novelinvestor.com/maxims-john-templeton/)).  
    This is the practical reason his 1939 basket could survive multiple total losses.

18. "by industry, by risk, and by country" - Templeton, 1993 ([Franklin Templeton reprint](https://www.franklintempleton.com/forms-literature/download/TL-R16)).  
    The diversification rule is multidimensional, not just a larger number of U.S. stocks.

19. "unpopular methods" - Templeton maxims, source mirror, 2001/Templeton Touch provenance ([Novel Investor](https://novelinvestor.com/maxims-john-templeton/)).  
    His process even applied contrarianism to methods: when a selection formula becomes popular, its edge decays.

20. "flexible, open-minded, and skeptical" - Templeton maxims, source mirror, 2001/Templeton Touch provenance ([Novel Investor](https://novelinvestor.com/maxims-john-templeton/)).  
    The three-part phrase captures his preferred mental stance: adaptable, curious, and resistant to fashion.

### Work, Risk Control, and Mistakes

21. "Do your homework" - Templeton, 1993 ([Franklin Templeton reprint](https://www.franklintempleton.com/forms-literature/download/TL-R16)).  
    Templeton's contrarianism was not anti-analysis. It required more work because the crowd would not validate the thesis early.

22. "Investigate before you invest" - Templeton, 1993 ([Franklin Templeton reprint](https://www.franklintempleton.com/forms-literature/download/TL-R16)).  
    This is the antidote to buying because a security is merely cheap, famous, or tipped.

23. "earnings or assets" - Templeton, 1993 ([Franklin Templeton reprint](https://www.franklintempleton.com/forms-literature/download/TL-R16)).  
    He framed stocks as claims on business economics rather than ticker symbols or stories.

24. "Aggressively monitor" - Templeton, 1993 ([Franklin Templeton reprint](https://www.franklintempleton.com/forms-literature/download/TL-R16)).  
    Long-term ownership did not mean neglect; he expected investors to react when facts changed.

25. "No investment is forever" - Templeton, 1993 ([Franklin Templeton reprint](https://www.franklintempleton.com/forms-literature/download/TL-R16)).  
    This is the clearest short sell-discipline quote in the opened primary source.

26. "Don't panic" - Templeton, 1993 ([Franklin Templeton reprint](https://www.franklintempleton.com/forms-literature/download/TL-R16)).  
    His crash advice was to reassess relative bargains, not reflexively liquidate after the fall.

27. "Learn from your mistakes" - Templeton, 1993 ([Franklin Templeton reprint](https://www.franklintempleton.com/forms-literature/download/TL-R16)).  
    Templeton treated errors as process data: forgive them, study them, and avoid compensating with bigger risks.

28. "This time is different" - Templeton, 1993 ([Franklin Templeton reprint](https://www.franklintempleton.com/forms-literature/download/TL-R16)).  
    The famous four-word warning is source-visible here, but later retellings often paraphrase it beyond the original sentence.

29. "no free lunch" - Templeton, 1993 ([Franklin Templeton reprint](https://www.franklintempleton.com/forms-literature/download/TL-R16)).  
    In his usage this covered tips, sentiment, IPO commission traps, and other shortcut temptations.

30. "Don't waste a minute" - Templeton, 1999 interview/profile ([William Green, Money PDF](https://williamgreenwrites.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/11/Sir-John-Templeton.pdf)).  
    Green uses the line near Templeton's comments on research intensity and spiritual inquiry; it also describes his general productivity ethic.

31. "more self-control" - Templeton, 1999 interview/profile ([William Green, Money PDF](https://williamgreenwrites.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/11/Sir-John-Templeton.pdf)).  
    Templeton linked emotional discipline directly to market success.

32. "careless and optimistic" - Templeton, 1999 interview/profile ([William Green, Money PDF](https://williamgreenwrites.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/11/Sir-John-Templeton.pdf)).  
    He used this to describe investors after big profits - a mirror-image risk to despair after losses.

33. "cautious and pessimistic" - Templeton, 1999 interview/profile ([William Green, Money PDF](https://williamgreenwrites.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/11/Sir-John-Templeton.pdf)).  
    The phrase captures the behavioral trap of becoming most fearful after prices have already fallen.

### Humility, Open Inquiry, and Spiritual Temperament

34. "How little we know" - Templeton Foundation motto, current as of 2026 ([John Templeton Foundation](https://www.templeton.org/about/sir-john-templeton)).  
    The Foundation presents this as Templeton's motto for both markets and philanthropy.

35. "eager to learn" - Templeton Foundation motto, current as of 2026 ([John Templeton Foundation](https://www.templeton.org/about/sir-john-templeton)).  
    The paired phrase matters because humility without curiosity would become passivity.

36. "new paths" - Templeton, official biography, current as of 2026 ([John Templeton Foundation](https://www.templeton.org/about/sir-john-templeton)).  
    The Foundation quotes him saying new paths were necessary to attain many goals.

37. "new spiritual information" - Templeton, official biography, current as of 2026 ([John Templeton Foundation](https://www.templeton.org/about/sir-john-templeton)).  
    This phrase belongs to Templeton's philanthropy and should not be forced into a narrow investment rule.

38. "rapid spiritual progress" - Templeton, 1999 interview/profile ([William Green, Money PDF](https://williamgreenwrites.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/11/Sir-John-Templeton.pdf)).  
    Green's profile shows how seriously Templeton took the spiritual-research project after selling the fund company.

39. "more scientific research" - Templeton, 1999 interview/profile ([William Green, Money PDF](https://williamgreenwrites.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/11/Sir-John-Templeton.pdf)).  
    This phrase is useful because it links his religious interest to inquiry rather than mere assertion.

40. "Begin with a prayer" - Templeton, 1993 ([Franklin Templeton reprint](https://www.franklintempleton.com/forms-literature/download/TL-R16)).  
    In the investing article, prayer is framed as a clarity and mistake-reduction practice. Transferability depends on the reader's own worldview.

## Annotated Primary and Near-Primary Materials

1. **"16 Rules for Investment Success" (1993; World Monitor, republished by Franklin Templeton).**  
   Best single source for Templeton's investing maxims: real return, anti-speculation, flexibility, global search, quality bargains, diversification, homework, monitoring, panic control, learning from mistakes, humility, and optimism ([Franklin Templeton reprint](https://www.franklintempleton.com/forms-literature/download/TL-R16)).

2. **John Templeton Foundation official biography (current page, accessed 2026-06-21).**  
   Best official source for the motto, "maximum pessimism" framing, 1939 basket summary, Templeton Growth Fund launch, 1954-1992 performance claim, philanthropy, knighthood, and death/status ([John Templeton Foundation](https://www.templeton.org/about/sir-john-templeton)).

   The Charlie Rose page identifies Templeton discussing *Worldwide Laws of Life* and labels the segment "Business, Books"; the site showed "See Transcript," but the transcript text did not render in the opened page, so this run uses it as an interview locator rather than a quote source ([Charlie Rose](https://charlierose.com/videos/26243)).

## Attribution Watchlist

- **"Bull markets are born on pessimism..."** This famous line did not appear in the opened primary or near-primary sources during this run. Keep it out of the quote index until an original venue is found.
- **"The four most dangerous words..."** The source-visible 1993 article supports a narrower sentence around "This time is different" and "among the four most costly words." Later versions should be labeled as paraphrases unless page-verified.
- **"Trouble is opportunity."** The phrase is widely associated with Templeton family retellings, but this run did not locate a primary Templeton venue. Treat as [attribution unverified] until page-verified.
- **Graham, Baruch, Will Rogers, and Hooper lines.** Templeton quoted them in "16 Rules," but they are not Templeton's own words. They are useful intellectual influences, not own-words entries.
- **Book-derived maxims.** The 22-maxims list is useful, but the opened source is a transcript/book mirror. Page-verify *The Templeton Touch* before relying on exact wording in later synthesis.

## Source-Quality Notes

The strongest quote source is the 1993 "16 Rules" reprint because it is explicitly by Sir John Templeton and republished with Foundation permission. The Foundation biography is official and strong for motto/status/legacy language, though promotional. William Green's Money profile is a high-quality secondary profile with direct interview quotations. Novel Investor is useful because it identifies source pathways, but its 1939 and dot-com passages should remain transcript-mirror evidence until the original Charlie Rose, Equities, and book sources are captured.

No letters-by-year archive surfaced for Templeton in this run. That absence matters: unlike Buffett, Marks, or Klarman, Templeton's accessible own-words corpus is concentrated in published rules, books, interviews, and later Foundation/Franklin republishing rather than a continuous shareholder-letter archive.

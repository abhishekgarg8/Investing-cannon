# Seth Klarman - In Their Own Words

As of 2026-06-21, Seth Klarman remains a living investor and active CEO/Portfolio Manager of Baupost. This file uses short, source-visible quote snippets rather than long excerpts, because many Klarman materials are private Baupost letters, paywalled interviews, or copyrighted books. Where a source is a media report quoting a private letter, the provenance is labeled rather than treated as a fully public primary document.

## Quote Index by Theme

### Margin of Safety, Risk, and Process

1. "standing apart from the crowd" - from the publisher/Google Books description of *Margin of Safety*, capturing the social isolation built into value investing ([Google Books, 1991](https://books.google.com/books/about/Margin_of_Safety.html?id=1QpjAAAACAAJ)).
2. "a very lonely undertaking" - the same *Margin of Safety* publisher text, useful because it frames discipline as psychological as much as analytical ([Google Books, 1991](https://books.google.com/books/about/Margin_of_Safety.html?id=1QpjAAAACAAJ)).
3. "volatility ... in your favor" - Klarman's 2010 CFA/Zweig interview on treating market swings as opportunity, not just pain ([Zweig/CFA Institute transcript, 2010](https://jasonzweig.com/why-one-legendary-investor-is-more-worried-than-ever/)).
4. "nothing great to do" - his explanation for why Baupost is not always fully invested ([Zweig/CFA Institute transcript, 2010](https://jasonzweig.com/why-one-legendary-investor-is-more-worried-than-ever/)).
5. "arrogance" - Klarman's deliberately uncomfortable label for buying when others sell and selling when others buy ([Zweig/CFA Institute transcript, 2010](https://jasonzweig.com/why-one-legendary-investor-is-more-worried-than-ever/)).
6. "temper it with ... humility" - the necessary antidote to contrarian confidence ([Zweig/CFA Institute transcript, 2010](https://jasonzweig.com/why-one-legendary-investor-is-more-worried-than-ever/)).
7. "entry point is what really matters" - his 2010 caution against treating long holding periods as a cure for overpaying ([Zweig/CFA Institute transcript, 2010](https://jasonzweig.com/why-one-legendary-investor-is-more-worried-than-ever/)).
8. "process, not outcome" - from Value Investor Insight's authorized 2009 excerpt of a Baupost annual letter; the phrase summarizes his Olympic-athlete analogy for investors ([Value Investor Insight, 2009](https://tilsonfunds.com/VIIOfSoundMind.pdf)).
9. "fearful overreaction equals opportunity" - from the same 2009 letter excerpt, describing where bargains arise in crisis ([Value Investor Insight, 2009](https://tilsonfunds.com/VIIOfSoundMind.pdf)).
10. "we might be wrong" - the humility clause in Klarman's uncertainty framework ([Value Investor Insight, 2009](https://tilsonfunds.com/VIIOfSoundMind.pdf)).
11. "healthy uncertainty drives" - his argument that doubt can improve diligence rather than paralyze it ([Value Investor Insight, 2009](https://tilsonfunds.com/VIIOfSoundMind.pdf)).
12. "intellectual honesty, rigor, creativity, and integrity" - the process virtues he says investing requires ([Value Investor Insight, 2009](https://tilsonfunds.com/VIIOfSoundMind.pdf)).

### Clients, Cash, and Institutional Design

13. "forgo some upside" - a 2018 Baupost letter excerpt reported by MarketFolly, summarizing Baupost's willingness to trade upside for protection ([MarketFolly, 2019](https://www.marketfolly.com/2019/01/seth-klarmans-baupost-group-year-end.html)).
14. "truncate the downside" - the same 2018 letter excerpt's plain-English risk-management objective ([MarketFolly, 2019](https://www.marketfolly.com/2019/01/seth-klarmans-baupost-group-year-end.html)).
15. "psychologically, algorithmically, and structurally" - Klarman's 2018 warning that markets can be leveraged without obvious balance-sheet borrowing ([MarketFolly, 2019](https://www.marketfolly.com/2019/01/seth-klarmans-baupost-group-year-end.html)).
16. "the rocket fuel ... will run out" - a Bloomberg/CNBC-reported phrase from his January 2020 investor letter, preserved here as a reported private-letter excerpt ([CNBC via Federal Bar Association PDF, 2020](https://www.fedbar.org/securities-law-section/wp-content/uploads/sites/129/2020/02/Hedge-fund-giant-Seth-Klarman.pdf)).
17. "interest rates go to die" - the same reported 2020 letter's phrase for a possible low-rate liquidity trap, especially in Europe ([CNBC via Federal Bar Association PDF, 2020](https://www.fedbar.org/securities-law-section/wp-content/uploads/sites/129/2020/02/Hedge-fund-giant-Seth-Klarman.pdf)).

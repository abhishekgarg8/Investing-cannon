# John Maynard Keynes - In Their Own Words

This file prioritizes Keynes's own published texts and source-visible excerpts from *The Collected Writings of John Maynard Keynes*, especially Volume XII for investment policy. Quote aggregators were not used as authority. The famous line usually rendered as "markets can remain irrational longer than you can remain solvent" remains excluded from the verified quote list: the search trail opened in this run led to quote sites that label it only as attributed or to modern pages discussing disputed attribution, not to Keynes's own writing.

## Money, inflation, and the fragile social contract

1. "Money is only important for what it will procure." - *Social Consequences of Changes in the Value of Money*, 1923 ([Economics Network](https://www.economicsnetwork.ac.uk/archive/keynes_persuasion/Social_Consequences_of_Changes_in_the_Value_of_Money.htm)).

2. "Each process, Inflation and Deflation alike, has inflicted great injuries." - *Social Consequences of Changes in the Value of Money*, 1923 ([Economics Network](https://www.economicsnetwork.ac.uk/archive/keynes_persuasion/Social_Consequences_of_Changes_in_the_Value_of_Money.htm)).

3. "What was deemed most secure has proved least so." - *Social Consequences of Changes in the Value of Money*, 1923 ([Economics Network](https://www.economicsnetwork.ac.uk/archive/keynes_persuasion/Social_Consequences_of_Changes_in_the_Value_of_Money.htm)).

4. "The process of wealth-getting degenerates into a gamble and a lottery." - "Inflation," 1919, later collected in *Essays in Persuasion* ([Economics Network](https://www.economicsnetwork.ac.uk/archive/keynes_persuasion/Inflation.htm)).

5. "There is no subtler, no surer means ... than to debauch the currency." - "Inflation," 1919 ([Economics Network](https://www.economicsnetwork.ac.uk/archive/keynes_persuasion/Inflation.htm)).

6. "A probable expectation of Deflation is bad enough; a certain expectation is disastrous." - "Alternative Aims in Monetary Policy," 1923 ([Economics Network](https://www.economicsnetwork.ac.uk/archive/keynes_persuasion/Alternative_Aims_in_Monetary_Policy.htm)).

7. "Gold is out of sight - gone back again into the soil." - "Auri Sacra Fames," 1930 ([Economics Network](https://www.economicsnetwork.ac.uk/archive/keynes_persuasion/Auri_Sacra_Fames.htm)).

8. "There are few Englishmen who do not rejoice at the breaking of our gold fetters." - "The End of the Gold Standard," 1931 ([Economics Network](https://www.economicsnetwork.ac.uk/archive/keynes_persuasion/The_End_of_the_Gold_Standard.htm)).

## Uncertainty, markets, and investment psychology

9. "It would be foolish ... to attach great weight to matters which are very uncertain." - *The General Theory*, Chapter 12, 1936 ([Marxists Internet Archive](https://www.marxists.org/reference/subject/economics/keynes/general-theory/ch12.htm)).

10. "Our knowledge ... some years hence is usually very slight and often negligible." - *The General Theory*, Chapter 12, 1936 ([Marxists Internet Archive](https://www.marxists.org/reference/subject/economics/keynes/general-theory/ch12.htm)).

11. "Business men play a mixed game of skill and chance." - *The General Theory*, Chapter 12, 1936 ([Marxists Internet Archive](https://www.marxists.org/reference/subject/economics/keynes/general-theory/ch12.htm)).

12. "The social object of skilled investment should be to defeat the dark forces of time and ignorance." - *The General Theory*, Chapter 12, 1936 ([Marxists Internet Archive](https://www.marxists.org/reference/subject/economics/keynes/general-theory/ch12.htm)).

13. "Worldly wisdom teaches that it is better for reputation to fail conventionally than to succeed unconventionally." - *The General Theory*, Chapter 12, 1936 ([Marxists Internet Archive](https://www.marxists.org/reference/subject/economics/keynes/general-theory/ch12.htm)).

14. "Enterprise becomes the bubble on a whirlpool of speculation." - *The General Theory*, Chapter 12, 1936 ([Marxists Internet Archive](https://www.marxists.org/reference/subject/economics/keynes/general-theory/ch12.htm)).

15. "Most ... decisions ... can only be taken as a result of animal spirits." - *The General Theory*, Chapter 12, 1936 ([Marxists Internet Archive](https://www.marxists.org/reference/subject/economics/keynes/general-theory/ch12.htm)).

## Keynes as investor and fiduciary

16. "The idea of wholesale shifts is ... impracticable and indeed undesirable." - 1938 King's College investment-policy memorandum, excerpted from *Collected Writings*, Vol. XII ([Novel Investor, 2019](https://novelinvestor.com/john-maynard-keyness-investment-policy/)).

17. "A careful selection of a few investments" - 1938 King's College investment-policy memorandum, excerpted from *Collected Writings*, Vol. XII ([Novel Investor, 2019](https://novelinvestor.com/john-maynard-keyness-investment-policy/)).

18. "A steadfast holding of these in fairly large units through thick and thin" - 1938 King's College investment-policy memorandum, excerpted from *Collected Writings*, Vol. XII ([Novel Investor, 2019](https://novelinvestor.com/john-maynard-keyness-investment-policy/)).

19. "Another important rule is the avoidance of second-class safe investments." - 1938 King's College investment-policy memorandum, excerpted from *Collected Writings*, Vol. XII ([Novel Investor, 2019](https://novelinvestor.com/john-maynard-keyness-investment-policy/)).

20. "One good share is safer than ten bad ones" - letter exchange with Francis Scott, excerpted from *Collected Writings*, Vol. XII ([Novel Investor, 2019](https://novelinvestor.com/john-maynard-keynes-on-concentration/)).

21. "I am quite incapable of having adequate knowledge of more than a very limited range of investments." - letter exchange with Francis Scott, excerpted from *Collected Writings*, Vol. XII ([Novel Investor, 2019](https://novelinvestor.com/john-maynard-keynes-on-concentration/)).

22. "I am generally trying to look a long way ahead." - letter exchange with Francis Scott, excerpted from *Collected Writings*, Vol. XII ([Novel Investor, 2019](https://novelinvestor.com/john-maynard-keynes-on-concentration/)).

23. "My objection is that I have no information on which to reach a good judgement." - letter exchange with Francis Scott, excerpted from *Collected Writings*, Vol. XII ([Novel Investor, 2019](https://novelinvestor.com/john-maynard-keynes-on-concentration/)).

## Politics, capitalism, and the future

34. "It is not true that individuals possess a prescriptive 'natural liberty' in their economic activities." - *The End of Laissez-Faire*, 1926 ([Economics Network](https://www.economicsnetwork.ac.uk/archive/keynes_persuasion/The_End_of-Laissez-Faire.htm)).

35. "I believe in Free Trade because ... it is the only policy which is technically sound." - "Am I a Liberal?" 1925 ([Economics Network](https://www.economicsnetwork.ac.uk/archive/keynes_persuasion/Am_I_a_Liberal.htm)).

36. "I sympathise with those who seek for something good in Soviet Russia." - "A Short View of Russia," 1925 ([Economics Network](https://www.economicsnetwork.ac.uk/archive/keynes_persuasion/A_Short_View_of_Russia.htm)).

37. "The decadent international but individualistic capitalism ... is not a success." - "National Self-Sufficiency," 1933 ([The Yale Review](https://yalereview.org/article/john-maynard-keynes-national-self-sufficiency)).

39. "We are suffering just now from a bad attack of economic pessimism." - "Economic Possibilities for our Grandchildren," 1930 ([Economics Network](https://www.economicsnetwork.ac.uk/archive/keynes_persuasion/Economic_Possibilities_for_our_Grandchildren.htm)).

41. "The art of life itself" - "Economic Possibilities for our Grandchildren," 1930 ([Economics Network](https://www.economicsnetwork.ac.uk/archive/keynes_persuasion/Economic_Possibilities_for_our_Grandchildren.htm)).

42. "The love of money as a possession ... will be recognised for what it is." - "Economic Possibilities for our Grandchildren," 1930 ([Economics Network](https://www.economicsnetwork.ac.uk/archive/keynes_persuasion/Economic_Possibilities_for_our_Grandchildren.htm)).

43. "The ideas of economists and political philosophers ... are more powerful than is commonly understood." - *The General Theory*, Chapter 24, 1936 ([Marxists Internet Archive](https://www.marxists.org/reference/subject/economics/keynes/general-theory/ch24.htm)).

44. "Practical men ... are usually the slaves of some defunct economist." - *The General Theory*, Chapter 24, 1936 ([Marxists Internet Archive](https://www.marxists.org/reference/subject/economics/keynes/general-theory/ch24.htm)).

## Attribution watchlist

- "Markets can remain irrational longer than you can remain solvent" remains [attribution unverified]. The opened search trail found Goodreads and The Quotations Page presenting it as community-added or "attributed," and a recent SimTrade article explicitly describes the origin as debated, with A. Gary Shilling sometimes claiming paternity. Do not use this line as a Keynes quote unless a future run finds a primary Keynes venue.
- Investment-policy quotations from 1934 and 1938 above are included because they are visibly excerpted from *Collected Writings*, Vol. XII, but the direct archive/book pages were not accessible in this run. Treat the Novel Investor pages as secondary-excerpt carriers; future F-key-writings should page-check Volume XII directly.
- Keynes's books and essays are often excerpted in anthologies with small wording differences. When a quote matters legally or analytically, prefer the Project Gutenberg, FRASER, Economics Network, Yale Review, or Cambridge/Collected Writings version opened during the relevant run.

## Annotated index of primary materials

1. [*Indian Currency and Finance* (1913), Project Gutenberg](https://www.gutenberg.org/files/49166/49166-h/49166-h.htm) - Early technical work on gold-exchange-standard mechanics; useful for seeing Keynes before his later anti-gold-standard turn.
2. [*The Economic Consequences of the Peace* (1919), Project Gutenberg](https://www.gutenberg.org/files/15776/15776-h/15776-h.htm) - Primary Versailles critique and the source of Keynes's postwar public reputation.
3. [*A Revision of the Treaty* (1922), Project Gutenberg](https://www.gutenberg.org/files/46037/46037-h/46037-h.htm) - Follow-up to *Economic Consequences*; documents Keynes's method of revising claims as new facts arrived.
4. [*A Tract on Monetary Reform* (1923), Project Gutenberg](https://www.gutenberg.org/files/65278/65278-h/65278-h.htm) - Core monetary-policy text; source for price stability, deflation, and gold-standard criticism.
5. [*A Treatise on Probability* (1921), Project Gutenberg record](https://www.gutenberg.org/ebooks/32625) - Primary uncertainty framework; important background for later "weight of argument" and long-term expectation language.
6. [*Essays in Persuasion* online table of contents](https://www.economicsnetwork.ac.uk/archive/keynes_persuasion/) - Convenient map to many Keynes essays; based on Project Gutenberg Canada transcription and useful for quote tracing.
7. ["Inflation" (1919)](https://www.economicsnetwork.ac.uk/archive/keynes_persuasion/Inflation.htm) - Short, forceful argument about currency debasement, social trust, and arbitrary redistribution.
8. ["Social Consequences of Changes in the Value of Money" (1923)](https://www.economicsnetwork.ac.uk/archive/keynes_persuasion/Social_Consequences_of_Changes_in_the_Value_of_Money.htm) - Best own-words source on investors, business owners, deflation, inflation, and money contracts.
9. ["Alternative Aims in Monetary Policy" (1923)](https://www.economicsnetwork.ac.uk/archive/keynes_persuasion/Alternative_Aims_in_Monetary_Policy.htm) - Shows Keynes choosing price stability over exchange-rate fixity when the two conflict.
10. ["The Economic Consequences of Mr. Churchill" (1925)](https://www.economicsnetwork.ac.uk/archive/keynes_persuasion/The_Economic_Consequences_of_Mr._Churchill.htm) - Primary attack on Britain's return to gold at prewar parity.
11. ["A Short View of Russia" (1925)](https://www.economicsnetwork.ac.uk/archive/keynes_persuasion/A_Short_View_of_Russia.htm) - Reveals Keynes's anti-Bolshevik but anti-money-worship moral lens.
12. ["The End of Laissez-Faire" (1926)](https://www.economicsnetwork.ac.uk/archive/keynes_persuasion/The_End_of_Laissez-Faire.htm) - Primary source for his rejection of metaphysical laissez-faire while avoiding blanket state socialism.
13. ["The Great Slump of 1930"](https://www.economicsnetwork.ac.uk/archive/keynes_persuasion/The_Great_Slump_of_1930.htm) - Crisis essay framing depression as a coordination and demand failure, not physical scarcity.
14. ["Saving and Spending" (1931)](https://www.economicsnetwork.ac.uk/archive/keynes_persuasion/Saving_and_Spending.htm) - Broadcast-style argument against private thrift as a depression cure.
15. [*The Means to Prosperity* (1933), Project Gutenberg Canada](https://gutenberg.ca/ebooks/keynes-means/keynes-means-00-h.html) - Policy pamphlet moving from diagnosis to loan-expenditure, multiplier logic, and recovery design.
16. ["National Self-Sufficiency" (1933), The Yale Review](https://yalereview.org/article/john-maynard-keynes-national-self-sufficiency) - Primary essay showing his nuanced, and controversial, move away from automatic economic internationalism.
17. [*The General Theory*, Chapter 12 (1936)](https://www.marxists.org/reference/subject/economics/keynes/general-theory/ch12.htm) - Essential investor text: long-term expectations, beauty contest, liquidity fetish, animal spirits, speculation versus enterprise.
18. [*The General Theory*, Chapter 24 (1936)](https://www.marxists.org/reference/subject/economics/keynes/general-theory/ch24.htm) - Closing social-philosophy chapter; source for socialization of investment and the power of ideas.
19. [*How to Pay for the War* (1940), FRASER/St. Louis Fed](https://fraser.stlouisfed.org/title/pay-war-6021/fulltext) - Late applied-policy text on scarcity, compulsory saving, family allowances, and war finance.
20. [1938 King's College investment-policy memorandum excerpt](https://novelinvestor.com/john-maynard-keyness-investment-policy/) - Secondary carrier for *Collected Writings*, Vol. XII; key for Keynes's mature investing rules.
21. [Francis Scott concentration-letter excerpt](https://novelinvestor.com/john-maynard-keynes-on-concentration/) - Secondary carrier for *Collected Writings*, Vol. XII; best accessible source for Keynes on concentration and knowledge limits.
22. [Cambridge ArchiveSearch - Papers of John Maynard Keynes](https://archivesearch.lib.cam.ac.uk/repositories/7/resources/1239) - Archive map for future primary verification of letters, investment files, and unpublished correspondence.
23. [Cambridge University Press - *Collected Writings of John Maynard Keynes* series](https://www.cambridge.org/core/series/collected-writings-of-john-maynard-keynes/76BAC759DE69633B2FE5A471646FE40E) - Official bibliographic anchor for the 30-volume edition; direct page checks remain needed.

No authentic Keynes podcasts exist. Later podcasts, YouTube summaries, and modern investor essays are secondary interpretation and should not be placed in this own-words index except as leads to primary material.

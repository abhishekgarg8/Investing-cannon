## Tier 1 / Near-Primary Sources

1. [CFA Institute - "On Low-P/E Investing" / A Conversation with John B. Neff, CFA (2006 PDF)](https://static1.1.sqspcdn.com/static/f/172986/1283427/1200961476880/John%2BNeff%2Band%2BLow%2BP-E%2Binvesting.pdf?token=7jFoO8zeFqMbWytb0ovDOXHz9BI%3D) - Essential first-person interview. Best source opened for Neff's process, underperformance periods, sell discipline, debt/yield thinking, and post-Windsor personal-account comments. Also states he managed Windsor and Gemini II.
2. [Google Books - John Neff on Investing bibliographic page](https://books.google.com/books/about/John_Neff_on_Investing.html?id=5-5cCgAAQBAJ) - Confirms the 1999 John Wiley book, co-author, scope, and diary-based structure. Use the book itself for Task F and quote verification; do not rely only on the preview copy.
3. [Internet Archive - John Neff on Investing metadata](https://archive.org/details/johnneffoninvest00john) - Confirms edition metadata, publisher, subject tags, ISBN, and access-restricted scan. Useful for locating page-level verification through controlled digital lending or library access.
4. [Vanguard Windsor Fund statutory prospectus (2026 PDF)](https://www.vanguard.com/pub/Pdf/p022.pdf) - Current primary fund document for Windsor's objective, active large/mid-cap value mandate, turnover, and modern multiadvisor structure. Use only for current vehicle context, not to infer Neff-era holdings.
5. [SEC N-CSR - Vanguard Windsor Fund annual report for fiscal 2021](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/107606/000110465921154138/tm2134327d1_ncsr.htm) - Primary filing showing modern Windsor reporting, advisors, benchmark, and performance table style. Useful template for finding older N-CSR/N-30D filings.
6. [SEC 497 supplement - Vanguard Windsor advisory restructuring (2012)](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/107606/000093247112004893/windsor497.htm) - Primary filing documenting Wellington's continuing role after AllianceBernstein was replaced by Pzena. Good for vehicle-structure caveats.
7. [SEC Amkor proxy biography of John B. Neff (2004)](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1047127/0000893220-04-001339.txt) - Primary public-company filing with concise biography: Neff as Windsor/Gemini II manager, Wellington senior vice president/managing partner, Chrysler director, Greenwich Associates board member.
8. [Dignity Memorial / Alleva Funeral Home obituary (2019)](https://www.dignitymemorial.com/obituaries/paoli-pa/john-neff-8734677) - Best opened source for exact birth and death dates and family details. Use with caution for career analysis, but it is strong for biographical dates.

## Tier 2 - Strong Institutional / Press Sources

9. [CFA Institute Enterprising Investor - In Memoriam: John B. [Philadelphia Inquirer - John Neff obituary (2019)](https://www.inquirer.com/business/john-neff-obituary-vanguard-windsor-funds-university-pennsylvania-endowment-fidelity-20190605.html) - Strong local business obituary with daughter confirmation of death, Windsor/Penn framing, and public character details. Useful for Bogle/Vanguard context and 23-of-31 figure.
12. [Wall Street Journal - John Neff Outperformed the Stock Market for Three Decades (2019)](https://www.wsj.com/articles/john-neff-outperformed-the-stock-market-for-three-decades-11560453159) - Obituary/profile with early-career narrative: post-Toledo New York job search, National City Bank, and transition to Wellington. Paywall-limited but opened enough to verify core biography.
13. [Barron's - Remembering Legendary Investor John Neff (2019)](https://www.barrons.com/articles/john-neff-51559786060) - Useful for cumulative 5,546% Windsor return claim and Barron's Roundtable context. Treat cumulative-return precision as requiring primary follow-up.
14. [Penn Gazette - An Eye for Value (1998)](https://thepenngazette.com/an-eye-for-value/) - Rich contemporaneous Penn profile with Neff quotes and endowment-management detail. Key source for Penn's portfolio transition, his low-P/E framing, and dry-spell examples.
15. [Wharton Magazine - School Update (1996)](https://magazine.wharton.upenn.edu/issues/fall-1996/school-update-5/) - Confirms creation of the John B. Neff Endowed Professorship in Finance and gives 1996 Penn endowment return context.

Useful for the 1974 Vanguard split, Wellington partnership history, Neff's role, and post-Neff Windsor capacity/brand caveats.
17. [WSJ - Windsor's Poor Performance Puts Dent in Wellington's Fees (1999)](https://www.wsj.com/articles/SB920852456762443500) - Useful critical/post-Neff source on Windsor's late-1990s value slump and fee effects. Do not treat this as Neff-era performance without careful date separation.

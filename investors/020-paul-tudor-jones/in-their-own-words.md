# Paul Tudor Jones - In Their Own Words

As of 2026-06-26T03:24:26Z.

## Source-provenance note

Jones does not appear to maintain a public, chronological archive of investor letters comparable to Berkshire, Oaktree, Pershing Square, or GMO. The best first-person corpus for this task is therefore a mosaic: Jack Schwager's 1989 *Market Wizards* interview, a few transcript carriers of older interviews, a 2020 Tudor BVI macro memo attributed to Paul Jones and Lorenzo Giorgianni, public speeches, podcast transcripts/pages, and reported apology or staff-memo excerpts. Where the carrier is a mirror or secondary article rather than the original publication venue, I flag that explicitly.

Because this file is a quote index, all exact quotations below are intentionally short fragments. The surrounding annotations explain the point without reproducing long passages from copyrighted books, articles, transcripts, or PDFs.

## Risk first, ego last

1. "Don't ever average losers" - In *Market Wizards*, this is the cleanest capsule of Jones's post-1979 cotton-loss doctrine: a bad trade is not a thesis to subsidize, it is an error to stop compounding. Source: [Schwager / Market Wizards PDF mirror, 1989](https://www.valueplays.net/wp-content/uploads/41775536-Market-Wizards.pdf).

2. "play great defense" - Jones presents defense, not prediction, as the most important trading rule; the point is to survive enough trades for edge to matter. Source: [Schwager / Market Wizards PDF mirror, 1989](https://www.valueplays.net/wp-content/uploads/41775536-Market-Wizards.pdf).

3. "I have a mental stop" - The stop is framed as a pre-commitment, not a suggestion to revisit under stress. Source: [Schwager / Market Wizards PDF mirror, 1989](https://www.valueplays.net/wp-content/uploads/41775536-Market-Wizards.pdf).

4. "Don't be a hero" - Jones's risk language is anti-macho despite the aggressive-trader reputation: ego is an operating hazard. Source: [Schwager / Market Wizards PDF mirror, 1989](https://www.valueplays.net/wp-content/uploads/41775536-Market-Wizards.pdf).

5. "I didn't think about what I could lose" - His own diagnosis of the 1979 cotton near-disaster is not that the market was unfair, but that his imagination was one-sided. Source: [Business Insider summary of Schwager interview, 2020](https://www.businessinsider.com/investing-strategy-paul-tudor-jones-shares-top-markets-trading-rules-2020-8).

6. "single most conservative investor" - In the TurtleTrader interview carrier, Jones defines conservatism as hatred of loss and liquidity discipline, not as low-volatility asset selection. Source: [TurtleTrader transcript carrier, n.d.](https://www.turtletrader.com/paul-tudor-jones-interview/).

7. "skewed reward-risk opportunities" - This is the positive half of his risk doctrine: take shots only when asymmetry justifies exposure. Source: [TurtleTrader transcript carrier, n.d.](https://www.turtletrader.com/paul-tudor-jones-interview/).

8. "minimum drawdown pain" - Jones repeatedly connects trade selection to the felt cost of drawdown; the emotional dimension is part of the risk system. Source: [TurtleTrader transcript carrier, n.d.](https://www.turtletrader.com/paul-tudor-jones-interview/).

9. "maximum upside opportunities" - The same interview pairs downside containment with enough upside to justify the trade. Source: [TurtleTrader transcript carrier, n.d.](https://www.turtletrader.com/paul-tudor-jones-interview/).

## Liquidity, price action, and market structure

10. "out when the market lets you out" - Jones credits Eli Tullis with teaching that size changes the exit problem; liquidity is not available on demand. Source: [Schwager / Market Wizards PDF mirror, 1989](https://www.valueplays.net/wp-content/uploads/41775536-Market-Wizards.pdf).

11. "market is going to go" - The fuller point is that the market ultimately goes where it goes, regardless of how visible a trader's hand is. Source: [Schwager / Market Wizards PDF mirror, 1989](https://www.valueplays.net/wp-content/uploads/41775536-Market-Wizards.pdf).

12. "let market price action guide" - In the 2020 macro memo, Jones says price action should guide decision-making first, with fundamentals often becoming clearer later. Source: [Tudor BVI macro memo mirror, 2020](https://www.lopp.net/pdf/BVI-Macro-Outlook.pdf).

13. "P&L always wins" - The memo's late-career version of humility: priors are subordinated to the profit-and-loss record. Source: [Tudor BVI macro memo mirror, 2020](https://www.lopp.net/pdf/BVI-Macro-Outlook.pdf).

14. "you can always get back in" - The phrase matters because exiting is not treated as intellectual defeat; it preserves optionality. Source: [Business Insider summary of Schwager interview, 2020](https://www.businessinsider.com/investing-strategy-paul-tudor-jones-shares-top-markets-trading-rules-2020-8).

15. "never trade" - Jones's rule against trading in uncontrolled situations, especially ahead of key reports, is less about being right than about refusing unpriceable risk. Source: [Business Insider summary of Schwager interview, 2020](https://www.businessinsider.com/investing-strategy-paul-tudor-jones-shares-top-markets-trading-rules-2020-8).

## Macro regimes and stores of value

16. "The Great Monetary Inflation" - The title phrase of the 2020 Tudor BVI memo marks Jones's pandemic-era regime call: fiscal-monetary coordination had changed the money backdrop. Source: [Tudor BVI macro memo mirror, 2020](https://www.lopp.net/pdf/BVI-Macro-Outlook.pdf).

17. "fastest horse" - His store-of-value framework in 2020 is comparative and performance-driven: own the best inflation hedge rather than become doctrinaire. Source: [Tudor BVI macro memo mirror, 2020](https://www.lopp.net/pdf/BVI-Macro-Outlook.pdf).

18. "Bitcoin futures" - The memo's operational hook was not a vague crypto endorsement but a Tudor BVI memo update permitting futures exposure. Source: [Tudor BVI macro memo mirror, 2020](https://www.lopp.net/pdf/BVI-Macro-Outlook.pdf).

19. "low single digit" - Jones capped initial bitcoin futures exposure at a low single digit percentage of net assets, consistent with his risk-first sizing culture. Source: [Tudor BVI macro memo mirror, 2020](https://www.lopp.net/pdf/BVI-Macro-Outlook.pdf).

20. "Bitcoin reminds me" - The memo compares bitcoin's setup to early gold exposure: a historical analogue, not a certainty claim. Source: [Tudor BVI macro memo mirror, 2020](https://www.lopp.net/pdf/BVI-Macro-Outlook.pdf).

21. "Why Bitcoin is the Best Inflation Hedge" - Colossus's 2026 episode timestamp labels Jones's current bitcoin discussion as part of his inflation-hedge framework, but the public page gates the full transcript. Source: [Colossus official episode page, 2026](https://colossus.com/episode/you-retire-you-die/).

22. "Bull Case for the Yen" - The same 2026 official show notes place Jones's current Japan/yen discussion inside the long-running macro-analogue playbook. Source: [Colossus official episode page, 2026](https://colossus.com/episode/you-retire-you-die/).

## Failure, learning, and late-career reflection

23. "Failure" - In the graduation-speech compilation, Jones makes failure the explicit topic rather than a decorative anecdote. Source: [Dear Graduates PDF compilation, 2020](https://12mv2.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/06/dear-graduates.pdf).

24. "secret to happiness" - The speech says service matters, while admitting the immediate lesson to ninth graders is failure and resilience. Source: [Dear Graduates PDF compilation, 2020](https://12mv2.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/06/dear-graduates.pdf).

25. "give you a tattoo" - Jones uses the tattoo metaphor for failure's permanence; it hurts, but it can engrave discipline. Source: [Dear Graduates PDF compilation, 2020](https://12mv2.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/06/dear-graduates.pdf).

26. "intellectual capital" - The same speech says his first Bed-Stuy education project failed because money without know-how was not enough. Source: [Dear Graduates PDF compilation, 2020](https://12mv2.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/06/dear-graduates.pdf).

27. "I lost my mama" - In the 2026 Invest Like the Best transcript carrier, Jones roots his kindness story in a childhood memory, not an abstract philanthropic theory. Source: [Podscan transcript carrier, 2026](https://podscan.fm/podcasts/invest-like-the-best-with-patrick-oshaughnessy/episodes/paul-tudor-jones-lessons-from-50-years-in-markets-invest-like-the-best-ep469).

28. "simple act of kindness" - That same story turns a childhood rescue into a lifelong philanthropic motif. Source: [Podscan transcript carrier, 2026](https://podscan.fm/podcasts/invest-like-the-best-with-patrick-oshaughnessy/episodes/paul-tudor-jones-lessons-from-50-years-in-markets-invest-like-the-best-ep469).

## Service, philanthropy, and accountability

29. "hole in my soul" - On *60 Minutes*, Jones describes philanthropy as filling a missing interior need, not just disposing of surplus capital. Source: [CBS News / 60 Minutes transcript, 2010](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/modern-day-robin-hood-60-minutes/).

30. "people in pain" - The CBS interview makes Robin Hood's mission concrete: poverty relief in New York, measured and funded through a grantmaking machine. Source: [CBS News / 60 Minutes transcript, 2010](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/modern-day-robin-hood-60-minutes/).

31. "offended, and I am sorry" - Jones's 2013 public apology after remarks about women traders and motherhood is a necessary part of the first-person record, because it shows his own language under reputational pressure. Source: [Washington Post, 2013](https://www.washingtonpost.com/local/education/paul-tudor-jones-my-remarks-offended-and-i-am-sorry/2013/05/24/6bb3f0c0-c4a9-11e2-6ee52d0eb7c1_story.html).

32. "I love you" - The reported Weinstein email opens with personal support that later became reputational evidence against Jones's judgment. Source: [Business Insider, 2017](https://www.businessinsider.com/paul-tudor-jones-says-its-good-news-everyone-will-forget-about-harvey-weinstein-2017-12).

33. "defended too long" - Jones's staff memo, as reported by Business Insider/CNBC, is the later self-critique: friendship had overridden judgment. Source: [Business Insider, 2017](https://www.businessinsider.com/paul-tudor-jones-says-its-good-news-everyone-will-forget-about-harvey-weinstein-2017-12).

## Annotated index of primary and near-primary materials

### Letters, memos, and authored materials

- **2020 - Tudor BVI macro outlook, "The Great Monetary Inflation."** Attributed to Paul Jones and Lorenzo Giorgianni, this is the most substantive public Jones/Tudor macro memo located in this run. It is essential for late-career inflation, money-creation, gold, bitcoin, price-action, and sizing language. Caveat: the accessible copy is a public mirror, not Tudor's own website. Source: [Tudor BVI macro memo mirror](https://www.lopp.net/pdf/BVI-Macro-Outlook.pdf).

- **1987/1994 and later editions - Foreword to George Soros, *The Alchemy of Finance*.** A table-of-contents scan confirms the first-edition foreword is by Paul Tudor Jones II. This should be page-checked from an authorized copy before quote extraction. Source located: [Dandelon/Wiley scan excerpt](https://external.dandelon.com/download/attachments/dandelon/ids/CH001B7F602EAAAC273C3C125766900600850.pdf).

- **No public annual investor-letter archive found.** Tudor is a private hedge-fund platform, and no reliable public chronological archive of Jones letters was found. Later Task F should treat filings, memos, speeches, interviews, and transcript carriers as the public writing/speaking corpus rather than assuming annual letters exist.

### Major interviews

- **1989 - Jack Schwager, *Market Wizards*, Paul Tudor Jones chapter.** The canonical interview for Jones's trading voice: 1979 cotton loss, 1987 crash context, risk controls, position sizing, flexibility, and trader psychology. Caveat: the accessible copy used here is a PDF mirror; page-check against an authorized edition before reusing exact quote strings at scale. Source: [Market Wizards PDF mirror](https://www.valueplays.net/wp-content/uploads/41775536-Market-Wizards.pdf).

- **Undated - TurtleTrader interview transcript carrier.** Useful for Jones's conservative self-description, futures/liquidity preference, and skewed reward-risk language. Caveat: original venue/date were not verified in this run. Source: [TurtleTrader](https://www.turtletrader.com/paul-tudor-jones-interview/).

- **2010 - CBS / 60 Minutes, "Modern-day Robin Hood."** Important first-person philanthropy interview, especially for how Jones narrates Robin Hood as a personal transformation and institutional grantmaking model. Source: [CBS News](https://www.cbsnews.com/news/modern-day-robin-hood-60-minutes/).

- **2026 - Invest Like the Best, "You Retire, You Die."** Current-status interview with Jones on kindness, trading versus investing, AI risk, yen, bitcoin, bubbles, daily routine, Robin Hood, and longevity. Caveat: Colossus exposes show notes and a partial transcript publicly, while full transcript access is gated; Podscan provides a fuller transcript carrier that should be checked against official audio. Sources: [Colossus official page](https://colossus.com/episode/you-retire-you-die/) and [Podscan transcript carrier](https://podscan.fm/podcasts/invest-like-the-best-with-patrick-oshaughnessy/episodes/paul-tudor-jones-lessons-from-50-years-in-markets-invest-like-the-best-ep469).

Caveat: the accessible source is a compilation PDF, not the original school or event page. Source: [Dear Graduates PDF](https://12mv2.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/06/dear-graduates.pdf).

### Official and filing context, not quote reservoirs

- **2003 - SEC-filed Tudor Fund for Employees L.P. final prospectus.** Not a Jones essay, but a primary filing that explains Tudor's trading methods, formal risk controls, conflicts, and Jones's role at the time. This anchors the interview claims about stops, active trading, and discretionary macro practice in operating disclosures. Source: [SEC prospectus](https://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/861895/000095013003003882/d424b3.htm).

- **Current - Tudor Investment Corporation official site.** Establishes the firm as an active macro/multi-strategy platform formed by Jones in 1980, but it is corporate description rather than Jones's own voice. Source: [Tudor Investment Corporation](https://www.tudorfunds.com/home).

- **Current - UVA Contemplative Sciences Center bio.** Useful for current role language: founder, co-chairman, CIO, controlling principal, principal risk taker, and primary risk taker for Tudor BVI Global. Source: [UVA profile](https://csc.virginia.edu/people/paul-tudor-jones).

- **1996 - SEC administrative order.** Primary legal context for the 1994 married-put / uptick-rule case. It should not be mined as Jones's own words, but it belongs beside his risk-control quotes as evidence that process and compliance failures still occurred. Source: [SEC order](https://www.sec.gov/files/litigation/admin/3437669.txt).
